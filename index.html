About

Hi there, my name is Tristan Santor, I am currently a student at University of Washington Bothell studying Computer Science and Software Engineering.

My passion for technology stems from me to oen day how technology works and interacts with another, how simple lines of code can ultimately impact an individual. I am mainly interested in the behind the scenes work such as backend and some full-stack development as well as Cybersecurity. I work with C++, Java and Python and currently learning the Qt framework to develop some awesome apps in the future.

Outside my software interests, I served as Activities Chair for FASA Sa UWB an active player at the UW Mens Volleyball Club in Seattle.

My Project Portfolio

My first project all Java. All visuals was interacted through the backend terminal where ASCII is what represents the vending machine. A simple game where you were able to buy drinks represented buy a letter (ex = S = Sprite) and have the options to drink, sell and buy. All dynamically working together. Overall multiple classes were involved which tremendously helped me understand the concepts of OOP. Check it out here: ASCII Vending Machine

Coming soon, currently developing a end-to-end encryption application that users can communicate with others without having their information being exposed.
